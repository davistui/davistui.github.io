Title:

**We're Still Not Shipping Faster**

I've sold app dev platforms and it's the same

Six months after go-live, the same call comes in. "We're still not shipping faster."

The platform works. It builds apps, it does what the demo showed, the dev team is quicker than it's ever been.

But they're not shipping faster. Shipping was never a tooling problem. It was a who-builds, who-governs, who-owns-it-after-launch problem — people, place, process — and they bought a platform hoping it would fix an operating model they never had.

The easy sale is the tech.

You demo the speed, map the features, close on "build faster." It feels like a win. You've sold velocity on top of a gap, and velocity just makes the gap arrive sooner.

A low-code platform can't build the operating model. It makes the one they've already got legible — fast.

So six months in they've got apps nobody governs, shadow IT they can't see, and the same bottleneck wearing a new badge. The platform didn't fail. It turned the lights on.

So I stopped selling the platform.

I used discovery to surface the gap first. Who owns governance. Who builds versus who approves. What happens to the app the day after launch. Slower deals, harder conversations, far fewer Q3 calls telling me it wasn't working.

The platform shows them how they work.

Your job is to show them first.
